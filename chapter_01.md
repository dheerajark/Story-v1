# Chapter 1: The Small Light

The noise in Sector 7 never stopped. It was a low sound that Jaxen Kross felt every night before he went to sleep. It was the sound of the big machine keeping three million people alive inside a rock in space. Today, the sound was a bit different.

Jax was in a small space behind his family's home. He had a small tool that made a light. He was looking at some broken wires and pipes.

"Don't tell Mom," a voice said from above.

Jax did not look up. He knew that voice. "Tell Mom what? That you are playing with the flight machines again, Cade? Or that you took the last food bar?"

Cade was seventeen. He was Jax's older brother. He jumped down into the small space. He was big and his jacket hit the walls. "I am not playing. I am learning to fly. It is important."

"Okay. But if the guards catch you, Dad will be angry," Jax said. He fixed a pipe. The pipe made a sound, and then it was okay.

"The guards will not catch me. I am fast," Cade said. "What are you doing? Is the air machine okay?"

"It was not working well. Now it is better," Jax said. He wiped his hands on his pants. "I fixed the water machine too. The water will taste good now."

"Jax! Cade! Dinner is ready!" 

It was their mother, Sarah Kross. She sounded very tired. She worked a lot to keep the power on in the colony.

Jax and Cade looked at each other. 

"Do you have the food bar?" Cade whispered.

Jax took a small food bar out of his pocket. He broke it in two pieces. He gave the big piece to his brother. "Go now. I will tell her you were helping me."

Cade took the food. He smiled a little. Then he went up the hatch.

Jax stayed in the dark for a minute. He liked the sound of the machines. But then, his light hit something in the corner. 

It was a small, black object. It was smooth and cold. 

Jax took it. He touched a button on the side. A small light came out of it. It showed a map of the stars. Jax had never seen this map before.

In the middle of the map, there was a red dot. It was far away from the asteroid.

"Jax! Come here now!" 

Jax put the object in his pocket. His heart was beating fast. The asteroid felt like a small room now. He wanted to see what was out there.
