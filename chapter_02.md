# Chapter 2: The Secret Signal

Dinner with the Kross family was quiet. Everyone was eating their soup. Marcus, the father, was tired. He had been working all day on the rocks.

"The work is harder now," Marcus said. "I have to work more hours starting tomorrow."

Sarah, the mother, was worried. "That is too much work, Marcus. It is dangerous out there."

"I have to do it," Marcus said. 

Cade looked at his father. "When I become a pilot, I will help us move to a better place. We will not stay here in the Gut."

"Just focus on your flying, Cade," Marcus said. 

Jax did not say anything. He could feel the black object in his pocket. He was thinking about the red dot on the map.

"Jax?" Sarah asked. "Is everything okay? Did you fix the machine?"

"Yes, it is fixed," Jax said. "I am going out to help a neighbor fix her heater."

"Stay close," Marcus told him. "The guards are everywhere today."

Jax went out, but he did not go to the neighbor's house. He went to a dark area of the station. He was hiding from the guards.

He found a quiet place and took out his tool and the black object.

"Show me the map," he whispered.

The map came out again. The red dot was closer now.

Suddenly, Jax heard a sound. Someone was behind him. 

A person in a cloak was standing there. The person had a sharp metal piece in their hand.

"That is not yours," the person said. The voice was like a machine.

Jax was scared. "I found it in the wall!"

The person stepped closer. It was a girl. She was not much older than Cade. Her eyes were a strange purple color.

"The signal is on," the girl said. "They are coming. If you want to save your family, you must help me."

Jax looked at her. "Help you do what?"

"We must turn off the shield," she said.

"Turn off the shield? We will all die!" Jax cried.

"We are already in danger, Jax," the girl said. "The leaders are not telling you the truth."
